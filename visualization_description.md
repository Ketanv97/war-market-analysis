1.	Oil vs Stock Market Graph (Normalized Prices)

How it looks:
X-axis: Date 

Y-axis: Normalized price (starting from 100) 

Lines: Oil, Nifty 50, S&P 500

What it shows:
When the war escalates, oil prices spike sharply. At the same time, stock markets dip (Nifty and S&P 500 go down). This visually confirms the negative correlation between oil prices and markets during crises.
Insight to highlight:
“Oil acts as a transmission channel: higher oil prices increase costs and investor uncertainty → market decline.”
2️. Correlation Heatmap
How it looks:
A square matrix of numbers and colors Shows correlation values (-1 to +1) between assets (oil, Nifty, S&P 500)
What it shows:
Strong negative correlation between oil and stock indices during the war. Positive correlation between Nifty and S&P 500 (global market moves somewhat together).
Insight to highlight:
“As oil rises due to geopolitical tension, stock indices fall — negative correlation is strong during crisis periods.”
3️. Daily Returns Graph
How it looks:
X-axis: Date 
Y-axis: Daily percentage return Lines for oil and stock indices
What it shows:
Extreme daily swings during the war period. Shows high volatility, not just price drop.
Insight to highlight:
“War doesn’t just lower prices—it increases market risk (volatility), which affects investor sentiment.”
4️. War Period Impact Graph (Zoomed-in)
How it looks:
Similar to the first graph but focuses only from Jan 2026 onwards
What it shows:
Clear spike in oil and simultaneous drop in Nifty and S&P 500 Easier to pinpoint exact war escalation impact
Insight to highlight:
“Focusing on the war period shows the immediate effect on markets, making the relationship between oil and stock drops more visible.”
5️. Sector Performance Graph
How it looks:
Lines for each sector stock: Reliance (Oil & Gas), Indigo (Aviation), ITC (FMCG) Normalized from 100
What it shows:
Oil & Gas (Reliance) goes up → beneficiary Aviation (Indigo) and FMCG (ITC) go down → negatively affected
Insight to highlight:
“Not all sectors are equally affected. Investors may shift to sectors benefiting from geopolitical events.”
